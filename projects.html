Thermodynamics of Running Shoes

November 2025

Applied Physics Research Paper

Analyzed running shoes as thermodynamic systems, examining energy management and heat transfer mechanisms in athletic footwear. Applied fundamental physics principles to understand how material properties affect both thermal comfort and mechanical performance.

Key Contributions:
  • Modeled running shoes as thermodynamic systems using energy balance equations and heat transfer principles
  • Applied the Einstein model to explain temperature-dependent behavior of midsole materials (EVA, TPU, PEBA)
  • Analyzed heat transfer via conduction, convection, and evaporation using Fourier's law
  • Evaluated experimental data on temperature distribution and material performance during running
  • Examined how carbon-fiber plates and advanced foam materials improve energy efficiency
